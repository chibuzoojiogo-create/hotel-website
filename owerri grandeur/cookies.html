Cookie Policy

Last updated: March 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Owerri Grandeur Hotels Limited uses cookies and similar technologies on our website. We believe in being straightforward about this — cookies help us give you a better experience, and we want you to understand exactly how.

1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, phone or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences and provide information to website owners about how their site is being used.

Cookies cannot run programmes or deliver viruses to your device. They are unique to your browser and are not personally identifiable on their own — though they may be combined with other information to identify you.

2 Types of Cookies We Use

We use four categories of cookies on our website:

Type Purpose Duration
Essential Cookies Required for the website to function. Enables booking forms, login sessions and security features. Cannot be disabled. Session / 1 year
Performance Cookies Help us understand how visitors interact with our website — which pages are most visited, where users drop off and how fast pages load. Data is anonymous. Up to 2 years
Functional Cookies Remember your preferences such as language, currency and previously entered booking details to save you time on future visits. Up to 1 year
Marketing Cookies Used to show you relevant advertisements on other platforms based on your interest in our services. Set by trusted third-party advertising partners. Up to 90 days

3 Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website are set by third-party services that appear on our pages. These include:

  • Google Analytics — measures website traffic and usage patterns (performance cookies)
  • Meta Pixel (Facebook/Instagram) — measures the effectiveness of our advertising (marketing cookies)
  • Google Maps — embedded maps on our Locations page (functional cookies)
  • Payment processors — Paystack and Flutterwave set essential cookies to process secure transactions

These third parties have their own privacy and cookie policies which we encourage you to review.

4 Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can control which non-essential cookies are placed on your device using the preference centre below. You can also manage cookies through your browser settings — most browsers allow you to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies or be notified when a new cookie is set.

Please note that refusing certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. Essential cookies cannot be disabled as the website will not work without them.
