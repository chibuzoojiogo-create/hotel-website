Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2025

At Owerri Grandeur Hotels Limited, your privacy is not a legal formality — it is a fundamental part of how we operate. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use and protect your personal information when you interact with us online, by phone or in person at any of our properties. By using our services, you agree to the terms outlined below.

1 Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us when you make a reservation, contact our concierge team, subscribe to our newsletter, or interact with our website. This includes:

  • Full name, email address, phone number and postal address
  • Payment information (processed securely — we do not store card numbers)
  • Reservation details including check-in/check-out dates and room preferences
  • Special requests, dietary requirements and accessibility needs
  • Passport or national ID information where required by Nigerian law
  • Communications you send to us by email, phone or through our website

We also automatically collect certain technical data when you visit our website, including your IP address, browser type, pages visited and the time of your visit. This data is collected using cookies — see our Cookie Policy for details.

2 How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Process and manage your reservation and payments
  • Communicate with you about your stay, including confirmations and updates
  • Personalise your experience based on your stated preferences
  • Send you promotional offers and estate news — only where you have opted in
  • Comply with legal obligations under Nigerian law, including NDPR (Nigeria Data Protection Regulation) requirements
  • Improve our website, services and marketing activities
  • Prevent fraud and ensure the security of our systems

3 Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR)

Owerri Grandeur Hotels Limited operates in full compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019, issued by the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA). Under the NDPR, you have the following rights:

  • The right to be informed about how your data is used
  • The right to access a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • The right to correct any inaccurate data we hold
  • The right to request deletion of your personal data
  • The right to object to processing of your data for marketing purposes
  • The right to withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the details below.

4 Sharing Your Information

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties. We may share your information with:

  • Payment processors and banks to handle transactions securely
  • Third-party service providers who support our operations (e.g. IT systems, email services) — all under strict data processing agreements
  • Government and regulatory authorities where required by Nigerian law
  • Our affiliated properties within the Owerri Grandeur group

5 Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss or destruction. Our website uses 256-bit SSL encryption for all data transmissions. Payment information is processed through PCI-DSS compliant systems. Access to personal data is restricted to authorised staff only.

While we take all reasonable precautions, no system is completely secure. If you suspect any unauthorised use of your data, please contact us immediately.

6 How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain your personal data only for as long as is necessary for the purpose it was collected, or as required by Nigerian law. Reservation records are typically kept for seven years for financial and regulatory purposes. Marketing data is retained until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent.

7 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. We will notify you of significant changes by posting a notice on our website or by email. Continued use of our services after such notice constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
