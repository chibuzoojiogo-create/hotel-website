Culinary Excellence

Dining at
Owerri Grandeur

Five distinct restaurants. One unwavering standard. From Igbo-inspired fine dining to the laid-back warmth of a Heartland evening terrace — every table is a story of Imo State.

Signature Restaurant

Imo Table

Our crown jewel. Imo Table takes its name from the river that gives our state its name — a tribute to the land that inspires every single dish. Executive Chef Chukwuemeka Obi fuses classical French technique with the bold, unashamed flavours of Igbo cuisine. The result is something Owerri has never seen before.

Think ofe onugbu risotto. Nkwobi-spiced wagyu. Abacha panna cotta. This is Igbo fine dining without apology — and without compromise.

All-Day Dining

The Verandah

Open-air dining overlooking the lush Owerri greenery. Fresh Imo State produce, light Nigerian classics and the kind of unhurried afternoon that the Heartland does better than anywhere else. Our most beloved spot for Sunday brunches.

Steakhouse & Grill

Oguta Grill

Named after the jewel of Imo State — Oguta Lake. Dry-aged cuts, open flame and the smoky satisfaction of a real grill. Premium Imo State goat sits alongside imported wagyu. For people who take their pepper soup and their steak equally seriously.

Igbo Cuisine

Mama's Kitchen

The most honest food in the building. Ofe onugbu, oha soup, ji mmiri oku, nkwobi, abacha and ugba — cooked the way your Imo State grandmother would, if she had a Michelin-trained sous chef. No pretence. No shortcuts. Just the real thing.

schedule 11:00 AM – 10:00 PM Reserve arrow_forward

Cocktail Lounge

The 1984 Bar

Named for the year we opened our doors in Owerri. Our head mixologist, Adaeze Nwosu, crafts cocktails that read like Imo State love letters — palm wine negronis, zobo spritzes, bitter kola old fashioneds, and ose oji martinis. Live highlife and jazz every Friday and Saturday from 9pm.
